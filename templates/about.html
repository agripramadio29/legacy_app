As a DevOps Engineer, I frequently found myself immersed in the repetitive task of configuring servers, especially when it came to addressing specific errors that necessitated restarting PostgreSQL servers and Apache Tomcat webservers. Despite having Ansible at my disposal, the monotony persisted, especially for my Windows-using colleagues who had to resort to installing third-party SSH clients for their tasks. This additional step only added to the inertia, making the entire process cumbersome and time-consuming. It was during one particularly frustrating commute, trapped in the notorious Cawang Traffic Jam, that the idea for this application dawned on me. Frustrated by the manual slog of restarting services via SSH, I recognized the urgent need for a more efficient solution. Thus, this project was born - an application meticulously crafted to streamline the service restarting process, thereby eradicating the need for tedious manual interventions. Its purpose? To simplify the lives of both my colleagues and myself, ushering in a new era of productivity and efficiency in our server management endeavors.

Currently, Legacy is only available for Linux Servers. Maybe in the future, I will build one for Windows Servers. Maybe if I'm not preoccupied with my office tasks, and stuck in traffic jams.
